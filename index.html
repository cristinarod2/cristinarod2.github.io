About Me

I am a Research Program Manager at UBC’s Molecular Imaging Research Facility, specializing in advanced preclinical imaging with a clear focus on PET, SPECT, and CT. My work bridges imaging science, radiochemistry, and quantitative analysis to enable high-impact translational research.

I design and lead complex in vivo and ex vivo imaging workflows, develop rigorous quantification pipelines, and collaborate closely with multidisciplinary teams to bring molecular imaging projects from concept to publication.

Beyond research management, I build custom Python tools and automated platforms that make laboratory workflows smarter, faster, and more reproducible — from tumour-tracking apps and radiotracer QC dashboards to 3D Slicer integrations and radioactive inventory systems.

Projects

Preclinical Imaging Platform

Development and optimization of PET/SPECT/CT protocols, quantitative imaging strategies, calibration frameworks, and standardized multi-time-point workflows for animal studies.

Custom Scientific Software

Creation of tailored Python applications for tumour volume tracking, imaging QC, radioactive inventory management, automated reporting, and data visualization — designed for real laboratory environments.

Hg-197m/g Theranostics

Method development for dual-isomer SPECT imaging, kinetic modeling, quantification workflows, and image-guided dosimetry for emerging radionuclide theranostics.
